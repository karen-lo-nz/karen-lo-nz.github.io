Intelligence: Emotional vs Intellectual

Emotional Quotient (EQ)

Emotional intelligence (EQ) refers to the ability to perceive, control, and evaluate emotions. It is defined as an individual’s ability to sense, understand and effectively apply the power and acumen of emotions to facilitate higher levels of collaboration and productivity. In a school environment, this are what institutions want students to gain in what they would all soft-skills i.e leadership, communication, collaboration which comes from either experiencing it or cultivating it by participating. There is no one right or wrong way to do it, but its a matter of growing that humane soft skills.

EQ is a skill is super attractive - that means, we can all learn and train and master it! Its not a rocket science/ a super difficult skill that requires born talent. It is achievable if we nurture and cultivate it.

EQ is centered on abilities such as:

  • Identifying emotions
  • Evaluating how others feel
  • Controlling one's own emotions
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication
  • Relating to others

Intellectual Quotient (IQ)

Intelligence Quotient (IQ) is a measure of what people know and how quickly they can solve problems using reasoning. But IQ tests alone don’t necessarily account for the full range of your thinking abilities. Your IQ usually refers to your intellectual ability. Similar to what you will learn in a school environment, the different subjects, theories and problem solving with a more clear cut approach.

IQ represents abilities such as:

  • Visual and spatial processing
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Working memory and short-term memory
  • Quantitative reasoning

Why is EQ important

The concept of EQ is relevant to the very core of being human - how we want to look after ourselves and others around us. Being aware and needs of a person either ownself or others will make life alot easier because the things that upsets and conflicts, are identified, resolved in a considerate and results in beneficial outcomes to all. It results in a "optimal" state of well-being i.e happiness, content, satisfy and peaceful.
